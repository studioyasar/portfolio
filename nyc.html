NYC Health+Hospitals
UX/UI Case Study/ Responsive Web Design
 
 
 
Project Overview
NYC Health + Hospitals is an integrated health care system of hospitals, neighborhood health centers, long-term care facilities, nursing homes, and home care – the public safety net health care system of New York City. Its’ mission is to deliver high quality health services with compassion, dignity, and respect to all, regardless of income, gender identity, or immigration status. This project covers an easy and quick booking process as weel as a minor style guide to contemplate NYC Health and Hospitals’ mission.
 
`
 
Background
The current website’s booking process is difficult, time consuming, and not efficient. Patients can only see doctors and locations, and have to make phone calls in order to make appointments. There is no a design process where patients can make appointments directly from website based on their availability.
 
`
 
Solution

Based on what users need the most, a booking appointment system was designed so users can book appointment directly from website based on their availability. After submitting

  • Specialty
  • Location
  • Date

    • Users are able to see:

  • All results based on their submission
  • Go through detailed information, select a doctor, and date and time
  • Submit the form and complete the process
 
`
 
Proto Persona
A proto persona was created in order to have better understanding of the current website. This was helpful since it gave an opportunity for collection of heuristics, market research, and intuition presenting opportunuties to articulate the target audience, their needs, and behaviors. The proto persona, Sumeyya Yurdakul is at the age of 28, and currently looking for a gynecologist near her. She has difficulties to access information, finding booking process difficult.
 
`
 
Defining User Path
The proto persona gave us an opportunity to see her pain points and goals. A user flow was created for her to see whether she could achieve her needs or not.
 
`
 
Heuristics Evaluation
  • Appearance/aesthetics: first impressions are important—they can make the difference between users staying or leaving your site
  • Content: users are at your site for the content—make it easy for them to find and use your site
  • Navigation: make getting around your site easy and eliminate the user's guesswork
  • Efficiency/functionality: following basic rules will keep user frustration to a minimum
 
`
 
Annotations & Red Lining
Navigation bar both on mobile and desktop is very busy and loaded with too much information which can overwhelm user and make navigation through process painful. It is locked to the top and takes almost half of the space on mobile devices. It is just waste of space. When scrolling up nav bar should disappear.
 
`
 
Card Sorting Analysis
Going through the entire links, we have found that the navigation bar was loaded with too many interconnected links which we found complicated and not user friendly. We worked on all of the pages and labeled some of the links to be moved under different categories or footer in order to clean up and come up with a neat and clean navigation bar.
 
`
 
Card Sorting + New Nav Bar

Card sorting each navigation bar links with relevant connection helped us to finalize the New Navigation Bar.

A free hand sketches helped us to design the process qucikly. We aimed to create a easy and simple booking process for users in couple steps.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

In this lo-fi prototype, we focused on:

  • A design system for Users to make appointments based on their availability.
  • Telling users what the institution stands for.
  • Presenting specialties on homepage in more visible way
  • Opportunity to see latest news in three categories
  • A profile for each doctor based on their location, hospital, specialty to make it easy for users to go to doctor they want.
  • Links to NYC Health and Hopitals locations. I + Google Maps Integration
  • MyChart Mock Ups, Newsletter Sign Ups, and a neat footer.
 
`
 
User Testing #1

Based on testing 5 users (these users were tested previously for the current website of NYC Health and Hospitals) here are some take outs:

  • Users liked the making appointment feature. They found it very helpful.
  • Users found adding hospitals, specialties and doctors to homepage helpful.
  • Appointment part could be developed. They found top part little confusing. Time and Date was not visible enough when opening the page.
  • Users found making appointment through MyChart a good idea since they were familiar with.
 
`
 
High-Fidelity Prototype

The main goal of this project was to provide a design system where people could make appointment based on their available schedule without long-wait phone calls. The Ui Styele tile was applied to to show users a realistic experience. Also, user testing insights were considered for redesign of some of the component.

 
`
 
User Testing #2

Based on testing 5 users, here are the take outs:

  • Making appointment part works.
  • The homepage is clean, neat and provides what users need the most.
  • Some of the design was not not visible to users.
  • The search box was faded.
  • Users found it difficult to find the date and time part after clicking on the submit button, because the page adjustment.
  • Users found the box idea for specialties, and card systems for doctors, and locations helpful.
  • Making appointments through MyChart was helpful, because users were already familiar with the content.
 
`
 
Iterations & Final Thoughts

This project was very fun to work with. Users find booking an appointment process difficult, time consuming, and not efficient. As a UI Designer, I created a design where users could book appointment directly from the website, based on their availability. If this design sytem could be applied to the current website, I believe it could solve a lot of problems both for users and the NYC Health and Hospitals.

  • It could save a lot of time of users.
  • It could save tons of money.
  • It could cancel unnecessary long-wait phone calls.
  • It could build a great trust between users and NYC Health and Hospital System. more and more...
