Green Elephant
UX/UI Case Study/ Application Design
 
 
 
Project Overview
Green Elephant is a New York City-based app to locally exchange used goods. New Yorkers want to reduce waste and save money but the process can be time consuming and cumbersome. Green Elephant makes the process of donating and receiving unwanted items easy and convenient. Green Elephant is a New York City-based app to locally exchange used goods.
 
`
 
Background
Many of us have that box in the corner or section of closet with a pile of items we’d like to get rid of. We all have perfectly good things… collecting dust. Often times you may be eager to get rid of your items. And you feel guilty about throwing them in the trash. But you also feel stressed about figuring out a time a place to drop off your unwanted items. And that donation bin down the block?
 
`
 
Solution
Well it’s overflowing..and you’re skeptical about where that stuff even ends up… That’s where Green Elephant comes in. Green Elephant allows you to… Create a post for a item you’d like to give away Search for free items to claim Message in-app to coordinate a pick-up or delivery of an item See your impact through financial savings
 
`
 
Proto Persona
We chosed our proto persona, Mary Smith as someone who is at the age of 38, a book publisher, and living in Brooklyn, New York. She feels good when donating. Just because she has very limited space, she wants to get ride of her daughter’s toys she no longer needs. She feels guilty when throwing them to garbage. She is looking for a platform where she can easily donate her stuff she no longer needs.

User Journey Map

Andrew Jones recently moved in with his fiance and needs to get rid of things that they do not need or do not fit their new small apartment. He is overwhelmed with the amount of items that could potentially end up in a trash and wants to find a way he could easily give these items away. Since he works from home most days, he is hoping to declutter his home quickly to make room for a proper work space. He experiences the following:

  • How am I going to donate all of these items instead of throwing them away?
  • Carrying all of these items on the subway is impossible.
  • I don’t want everyone on social media to see the items I am donating.
  • There has to be someone in my building or neighborhood that could use these items.
  • This app for New Yorkers will help find my unwanted things a new home.
  • What if these items ultimately end up in the trash?
  • Looks like there are other users in this aare who want used furniture.
  • Sad to let go of some of his items.
  • Share Application with friends and family so that they can easily donate and find used items.

User Flow

Based on the purpose of the application a user flow was created. In this flow, users will

  • Sign in / Sign up.
  • Create a profile.
  • Go through home page.
  • Post and search an item.
  • See notifications, messages.
  • See their impact.

These paper sketches were created by diffrent individuals of the team. My role as a UX/UI designer was to create

  • Impact pages.

After completing all parts, paper sketches were put together for a low-fidelity prototype.

First User Testing

The Low-Fidelity prototype was tested on six users. Some of the outcomes are as follows:

  • Home Screen - took 1 user 3 min to find a Plus button
  • No ability to go back, reach Home, Messages from every frame
  • Identical Homepage icons: profile, messages, post, search for item
  • Expiration date made sense
  • Impact part increased trust for users
  • Gifted was a confusing terminology
  • Confused about setting location
 
`
 
User Flow Iteration

After very helpful user testings, user flow partially was redeigned in order to meet users’ needs. Some changes are as follow:

  • Simplified Sign in / Sign up pages
  • Texts were added to icons in order to indicate what they stand for
  • Go back buttons were created for every page needed.
  • The wording, language and terminology were redesigned.
  • Detailed info were added some part of the application to prevent confusion.
 
`
 
High-Fidelity Prototype

In order to get detailed feedback on certain elements of the design that would not be possible with pen and paper, the high fidelity prototype was created. In this hi-fi

  • Clickable links and menu were created.
  • Realistic visual hierarchy, priority of screen elements, and screen size were redesigned.
  • Graphical elements, style tile were created.
  • Pictures were added.
  • The logo was added.
 
`
